Highest-frequency traps on the real WA DOL exam
🛑
4-way stop tie-breaker
Same-time arrivals: yield to the car on your right. Opposite directions going straight: both can go. Turning left always yields to oncoming straight traffic — even if you arrived first.
Most tested
⏱
3-second following distance
Pick a fixed point. When the car ahead passes it, count 3 seconds. If you pass the point before counting to 3, you are too close. Double to 6 seconds in rain, fog, or ice.
Most tested
🌧
Wipers on = headlights on (WA law)
Washington law requires headlights whenever your wipers are running due to weather. Not a suggestion — it is a statute. Appears on almost every version of the exam.
WA-specific
🍺
Know all three BAC limits
Adults 21+: 0.08%. Commercial drivers (CDL): 0.04%. Drivers under 21: 0.02%. Implied consent means by driving you agree to be tested. Refusing = automatic license suspension.
Most tested
🚌
School bus red lights — both directions
Stop in both directions when a school bus has flashing red lights — except on roads divided by a physical median (concrete wall or raised island). A painted center line does NOT count.
WA-specific
🚸
School zone: 20 mph when kids are present
You must slow to 20 mph when children are present — not just when the flasher is active. If you see kids near the roadway by a school, 20 mph is required regardless of the signal.
Common trap
🟡
Yellow light = stop if you safely can
The exam definition: a yellow light means the signal is about to turn red — slow down and stop if you can safely do so. "Speed up to make it" is always the wrong answer on the exam.
Common trap
➡
Passing on the right is usually illegal
In WA, passing on the right is only allowed when the road has two or more lanes going the same direction, or when the car ahead is turning left. Using the shoulder to pass is always illegal.
WA-specific
🔵
Sign colors: blue = services, brown = recreation
Blue signs mark services (gas, food, hospitals). Brown signs indicate parks and recreation. Green = directions and exits. Orange = construction zones. Yellow = warnings. White = regulations.
Common trap
⛽
Only time sobers you up
Coffee, food, water, cold air, and exercise do not reduce your BAC or restore driving ability. Only time eliminates alcohol from your system. The exam always lists these as distractors.
